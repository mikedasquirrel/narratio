### The Hierarchy of Forces

Across domains, strength ranking:

**1. λ (Fundamentals): Strongest when present**
- Aviation: λ = 0.833 → Complete dominance
- Sports: λ = 0.750-0.800 → Physical laws win
- Can't be overcome by narrative alone

**2. θ (Awareness): Strong in educated populations**
- Dominates in 7/12 domains
- Educated consumers resist narrative manipulation
- Free will is real and measurable

**3. ة (Nominative Gravity): Operates when others are weak**
- Manifests in low-awareness contexts (hurricanes)
- Manifests in low-barrier contexts (crypto)
- Real but often suppressed

### The Prestige Exception

In domains where **narrative evaluation IS the explicit task**:

**θ flips from suppression to amplification**

Examples:
- Academy Awards (judging film quality/prestige)
- Literature prizes (judging writing quality)
- Restaurant critics (judging dining experience)
- Art competitions (judging aesthetic merit)

**Formula changes:** `Д = ة + θ - λ`

Awareness becomes **legitimization** instead of resistance.

### Boundary Conditions for Narrative Manifestation

Nominative effects manifest when:

```
ة > θ + λ

Requires:
1. High п (narrativity > 0.6)
2. Strong ة factors (branding, cultural salience)
3. Low awareness (θ < 0.3) OR prestige domain
4. Low barriers (λ < 0.4)
```

**Only 2 of our 12 domains meet these conditions** (Crypto, Hurricanes).
