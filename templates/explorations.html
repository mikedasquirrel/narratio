validated research domains

Domains that passed through the universal pipeline (Nov 2025) but aren't betting systems. These validate narrative theory in non-market contexts.

Hurricanes
dual π validated
π: Storm 0.30 / Response 0.68 | n=819
Finding: Name gender and phonetic properties affect evacuation decisions. Female-named hurricanes show lower evacuation intent (34% vs 56% for male names). Validates nominative determinism in life-death contexts.
Dual π framework: Storm itself is physics-constrained (0.30), but human response is moderately narrative (0.68). Names shouldn't matter for physics, but they matter for perception and decisions.
Supreme Court
r = 0.785
π: 0.52 | R² = 61.6% | n=26 opinions
Finding: Narrative quality predicts forward citations. Better-written opinions get cited more often. Works in evidence-weighted adversarial system.
Moderate narrativity (π=0.52) in legal domain. Evidence matters, but how you tell the legal story affects long-term impact. Narrative doesn't determine outcome, but influences legacy and citation.
Startups
marginal (n=258)

High π (0.76) suggests narrative should matter, but market reality constrains (κ low). Good products enable good stories, but you can't narrative a bad product into success. Needs 1000+ samples for robust validation.

what we've learned

Framework Validation

The Spectrum is Real: From π=0.04 (lottery) to π=0.974 (WWE), we see continuous gradation from pure physics to pure narrative. No sharp boundaries - domains blend along spectrum.

High π ≠ High Agency: UFC (π=0.72, Δ=0.025) and Poker (π=0.835, R²=0.047) show that narrative openness doesn't guarantee narrative influence. Constraints (λ) and variance matter.

Dual π Framework: Hurricanes validated split narrativity - storm physics (π=0.30) vs human response (π=0.68). Same domain, different aspects, different narrative potential.

Sample Size Matters: Startups (n=258) shows need for large samples. Tennis (n=74,906) and Golf (n=5,000) have robust conclusions. Small n requires more skepticism.

Context is Everything: Sub-domain optimization reveals 3x-5x stronger effects than generic analysis. Horror films, 1980s movies, heavyweight boxing show context-specific narrative rules.

Bookends Validate: Clean null at lower bound (lottery) and strong signal at upper bound (self-rated, WWE) confirm we're measuring real phenomenon, not systematic bias.
